The Variations Condominium Association, Inc

Bylaw Change Information: Capital Fund/Initiation Fee

In 2015 and 2016, the Board of Directors approved an amendment to our Bylaws. It has not passed either year due to not having quorum on the vote. Many stated that they did not understand the Bylaw change before the vote.

Explanation

The Bylaw change says that the new homeowner, at time of closing, will pay the amount of 2 months HOA dues to the Variations Home Ownerâ€™s Association (currently $720). For perspective, based on sales just the last two summers, this bylaw change would have added approximately $12,960 to the bottom line to the Reserve Funding.

Why is this important to the Variations?

The goal of this Bylaw change is to:

  • Increase our reserves which are needed for repair of an aging community.
  • Gives new homeowners the opportunity to contribute to the enhancements already done

Endorsements

Rob Stein, our attorney for many condominium associations, states that this is standard for condo associations.

Marc Nicholson, Variations resident and real estate broker with ReMarcable Real Estate Services, states:

"It (Initiation Fee) is common for most communities and the buyers do not push back on it EVER. I have never seen the Seller have to pay it. It could, of course, be negotiated into the contract, but it is the new standard in the industry."
